# MyMedic — Product & Market Roadmap

## 1. Roadmap Philosophy

The MyMedic roadmap is sequenced around **operational adoption** rather than feature accumulation.

The priority order is:

> **Slot Utilization → Yield → Retention → Expansion → Interoperability**

This mirrors how revenue and workflows unlock in outpatient healthcare.

---

## 2. Phase Roadmap (High-Level)

### **Phase 1 — Clinics (0–18 months)**
Primary value drivers:
- utilization
- yield
- payment flow
- scheduling
- follow-ups

Modules:
- scheduling (S1)
- PM3 payments (deposit + prepaid)
- patient portal (hybrid)
- WhatsApp integration
- doctor mobile calendar (M2)
- chain pricing (hybrid)
- reconciliation & receipt books
- basic analytics

Outcomes:
- solves no-shows
- reduces cancellations
- improves follow-up rates
- increases yield per doctor
- increases staff throughput
- establishes financial lock-in

---

### **Phase 2 — Chains (18–36 months)**

Primary value drivers:
- multi-branch ops
- chain governance
- finance control
- centralized procurement
- standardization

Modules:
- chain dashboards + reporting
- multi-branch hierarchy
- staff permissions & ABAC
- centralized pricing catalog
- chain analytics (DAC)
- onboarding system for chains
- ledger + refund systems
- packages + subscriptions (optional)

Outcomes:
- clinics → chains expansion model
- higher ARPC
- enterprise sales motion begins
- procurement compatibility
- chain-level SSO (optional)

---

### **Phase 3 — Hospital OPD (36–60 months)**

Primary value drivers:
- OPD throughput
- referral pathways
- follow-up adherence
- admission conversion
- insurance readiness

Modules:
- ADT (Admission/Discharge/Transfer) interface
- OPD → IPD referral workflows
- order sets (procedures + labs)
- OPD billing integration
- tele consult + follow-up packages
- TPA interface (insurance)
- care pathways
- claim data export

Outcomes:
- outpatient wedge extends into inpatient workflows
- unlocks enterprise ACV (₹15L–₹2Cr/yr)
- HIS interoperability layer forms

---

### **Phase 4 — HIS Interop (60–84 months)**

Primary value drivers:
- insurance capture
- billing compliance
- PACS/RIS connectivity

Modules:
- HIS/EMR adapters
- PACS/RIS connectors
- claims clearinghouse (India-specific)
- materials management (OT/day-care)
- ICD/CPT/DRG mapping (if needed)

Outcomes:
- becomes outpatient + revenue OS for hospitals

## 4. Geographical Expansion Roadmap

Expansion aligns with outpatient digital maturity:

1. **India**
   - WhatsApp-first
   - UPI-first
   - cash-pay & elective heavy

2. **MENA (KSA + UAE)**
   - high private outpatient density
   - insurance heavy
   - OPD/hospital friendly

3. **SEA (ID + VN + PH)**
   - fragmented care
   - mixed cash-pay & insurance
   - chain formation under way

4. **LatAm (MX + BR)**
   - clinic consolidation
   - cosmetic/aesthetic segments strong

## 6. The “Outpatient OS” Vision

Goal state:

- clinics run on MyMedic
- chains expand on MyMedic
- hospitals integrate MyMedic
- patients return via MyMedic
- insurers trust MyMedic claims data

This creates a **data + workflow + financial moat** that is large and durable.
